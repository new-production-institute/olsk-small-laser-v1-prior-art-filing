# OLSK Small Laser V1

![OLSK Small Laser](media/OLSK-Small-Laser.png)

*Open Source Laser Cutter – Small version – Open Lab Starter Kit*

---

## Introduction

The OLSK Small Laser V1 is a compact but powerful desktop laser cutter. It features the design of the **[Fabulaser Mini](https://github.com/fab-machines/Fabulaser-Mini)**, developed to fit in Fab Labs and classrooms, with a compact size of 870×810×380 mm and a weight under 60 kg. It uses an optimized 40 W CO₂ laser, capable of cutting 8 mm acrylic. With a cutting area of 600×400 mm, it is one of the largest machines in its category.

![Highlights](media/Highlights.png)

---

## Learning by Making

The assembly of the OLSK Small Laser provides a unique learning experience. With the help of a workshop and the assembly manual, this laser cutter can be built in a few days, allowing participants to understand the role of each part and how the mechanics, optics, and electronics function from the inside out.

![Workshop](media/HH_T.jpg)

---

## The Assembly Manual

![Manual](media/Manual2.png)

Just like the **[Fabulaser Mini](https://github.com/fab-machines/Fabulaser-Mini)**, the OLSK Small Laser V1 comes with a detailed building manual. Divided into logical steps, the manual also instructs on general building practices, laser calibration, and how to connect all electronics. Teachers can also use the manual to divide participants into groups for workshops.

---

## Specifications V1

- **Laser source:** 40 W CO₂
- **Cutting area:** 600×400 mm
- **Frame:** 30×30 mm aluminum profiles
- **Resolution:** 0.05 mm
- **Max cutting thickness:**
  - 8 mm acrylic
  - 6 mm MDF
  - 8 mm plywood
- **Max speed:** 400 mm/s
- **Motion:** linear rails
- **Housing:** aluminum composite
- **Bed:** aluminum lamella
- **Controller:** 32-bit LPC1768
- **Firmware:** grbl-LPC
- **Machine dimensions:** 870×810×380 mm
- **Motion system:** GT2 10 mm belts
- **Guides:** 12 mm linear rails
- **Motors:** NEMA 17 steppers
